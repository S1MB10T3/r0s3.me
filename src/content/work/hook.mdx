<Intro>
  Hook is an options trading platform where experienced derivatives traders
  and complete newcomers share the same product. As the sole product designer
  I led everything end to end: V1, the Odyssey redesign on desktop and
  mobile, the design system, and the brand.
</Intro>

<Section title="odyssey">
  ![Odyssey desktop trading interface](media/hook/odyssey-desktop.png)

  The Odyssey redesign was the centerpiece. Desktop had to be dense enough
  for an active trader to scan markets and execute fast, yet legible enough
  that a first-timer could read the order ticket without bailing. Risk
  visualization sat inside the ticket itself, so users saw their asymmetric
  exposure before committing.

  ![Odyssey mobile PWA flows](media/hook/odyssey-mobile.png)

  The mobile PWA compressed the desktop flow into a native-feeling app
  without dropping the educational scaffolding. Education surfaced
  contextually inside the flow instead of a separate onboarding, so new users
  learned options while they traded. Post-launch, Odyssey doubled daily
  actives and lifted retention 47%.
</Section>

<Section title="designsystem">
  ![Sayreville Strata design system cover](media/hook/design-system-cover.png)

  Sayreville Strata is the design system underneath the redesign, named for a
  Michelle Stuart piece built from layered brick and rag paper. Built on a
  Material UI base and extended into composable primitives, every Hook
  surface is a different arrangement of the same blocks.

  <div className="page-media-row">
    ![Color palette](media/hook/colors-palette.png)

    ![Status colors](media/hook/colors-statuses.png)
  </div>

  Hook's brand color is orange, one of the hardest cases for WCAG contrast
  the moment it touches text on a light background. I tested every approved
  color combination per theme and locked the tokens so each surface passed AA
  without losing the brand. The result stayed unmistakably Hook across
  desktop, mobile, and marketing.

  Guidelines documented the reasoning behind each decision, not just the
  values, so the system stayed consistent as new contributors joined.
</Section>

<Section title="branding">
  ![Perps launch teaser](media/hook/perps-teaser.png)

  The brand had to read as a serious financial product without defaulting to
  the terminal-dark aesthetic that dominates the category. Perps, puts, and
  calls got a warmer, print-inspired identity instead: orange, editorial
  type, and newspaper texture that made Hook feel approachable and rigorous
  at once, in product and in marketing.

  ![Print-inspired brand applications](media/hook/branding.png)
</Section>

<Postmortem>
  Hook was end-to-end ownership: research, product, system, and brand from
  one seat. The Odyssey redesign doubled daily active users, lifted retention
  47 percent, and set the company's single-day volume record at three times
  the previous peak.
</Postmortem>
