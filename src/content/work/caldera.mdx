<Intro>
  Caldera is an infrastructure company that lets teams launch and run their
  own custom rollups. As Senior Product Designer I led design across the
  deployment flow, bridge widget, hub pages, and admin dashboard, plus a full
  company rebrand.
</Intro>

<Section title="designsystem">
  ![Katmai design system cover](media/caldera/design-system-cover.webp)

  The design system had one job: stay unmistakably Caldera while flexing to
  any customer's brand. Every surface, from the bridge widget to hub pages,
  needed to inherit a partner's colors, type, and corner radii without
  breaking a single component.

  <div className="page-media-row">
    ![Core color palette](media/caldera/colors-palette.webp)

    ![Status and semantic colors](media/caldera/colors-statuses.webp)
  </div>

  The color system leaned on semantic tokens instead of raw hex values, so
  swapping in a partner's palette restyled statuses, surfaces, and data viz
  in one move. The goal was a system that stayed visually Caldera by default
  but let a customer's brand take over completely without anyone touching a
  component.

  To stress-test theming end to end, I built a custom Figma plugin that
  auto-generated the bridge widget across color, radius, shadow, and
  typography variations.

  ![Bridge widget theme variations generated by the Figma plugin](media/caldera/widget-variations.webp)
</Section>

<Section title="artdirection">
  ![Caldera rebrand identity spread](media/caldera/rebrand.webp)

  The rebrand set a new visual direction for Caldera: a hotter palette,
  denser texture, and a mark that could survive everything from conference
  banners to terminal output. I led a graphic designer and an external
  contract team, in partnership with marketing, to ship the logo, identity,
  marketing site, and social system.
</Section>

<Section title="widget">
  ![Metabridge widget](media/caldera/metabridge.webp)

  Metabridge is the customer-facing bridge widget, supporting 30+ networks
  with true cross-chain pairs across more than 10 of them. It had to feel
  native inside any partner's site, so every piece of it (color, type,
  radius, shadow) is themeable from a single config.

  ![Bridge builder configuration flow](media/caldera/bridge-builder.webp)

  The bridge builder let partners assemble and theme their own bridge without
  a designer in the loop. Drop in a logo and a palette, preview the widget
  live, and ship a branded bridge that still behaves exactly like every other
  Caldera deployment.
</Section>

<Section title="hub">
  ![Partner hub page template](media/caldera/hub.webp)

  Hub pages gave every partner a templated landing page for their rollup:
  docs, bridge entry points, and ecosystem links in one place. The template
  had to scale across dozens of partners while still feeling owned by each
  one.
</Section>

<Section title="dashboard">
  ![Rollup Engine dashboard](media/caldera/dashboard.webp)

  The Rollup Engine dashboard is Caldera's administrator-facing surface. The
  redesign consolidated workflows that previously lived across multiple
  external sites and tools into a single interface, and rolled out as the
  default across every customer account.
</Section>

<Postmortem>

</Postmortem>
